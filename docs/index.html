Questions founders actually ask

Will my customers know I'm using a third party?

No. Dunning emails are sent under your brand, and retries look like any other charge attempt from your Stripe account. Your customers never see the name RecoverFlow.

What access do you have to my Stripe account?

Read access to subscriptions and invoices via Stripe Connect OAuth, plus the ability to retry failed invoices. You can revoke it from your Stripe dashboard at any time, and we never see or store card numbers — Stripe holds those.

Doesn't Stripe already retry failed payments?

Stripe's Smart Retries help, but they're generic. RecoverFlow classifies each decline code and reacts differently — payday-aware retries for insufficient funds, immediate card-update emails for expired cards, backoff for processing errors — and layers branded dunning sequences on top. The two coexist fine; we simply recover what slips through.

How is the 25% calculated and billed?

We attribute every recovery to the retry, card update, or email that caused it — payments that came back on their own are never counted. At the end of each month you get an invoice for 25% of attributed recoveries ($29 minimum after your first 30 days).

Could retries annoy my customers or trigger disputes?

Retries are spaced deliberately and capped — never blind, back-to-back attempts. Hard declines (stolen card, closed account) are never retried at all, which is exactly the behavior that avoids disputes.

What happens if I cancel?

Disconnect RecoverFlow from your Stripe dashboard in one click. Nothing breaks, nothing changes in your billing, and you owe nothing beyond fees on payments already recovered.
